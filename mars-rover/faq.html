Exploring Mars with NASA’s Rovers

Mars is a cold, dry, and rocky world that has fascinated scientists for centuries. As the fourth planet from the Sun, it is one of Earth’s closest planetary neighbors and one of the easiest planets to spot in the night sky, appearing as a distinct reddish point of light. Despite its harsh environment, Mars remains one of the most studied worlds beyond Earth.

NASA has been exploring Mars for decades using robotic rovers designed to study its surface, climate, and history. Beginning with the Mars Pathfinder mission in 1997 and continuing through advanced missions like Curiosity and Perseverance, each rover has expanded our understanding of the Red Planet and revealed evidence that water once flowed across its surface.

Together, these missions continue to uncover clues about Mars’ past, test new technologies for future exploration, and prepare the way for eventual human missions. This site introduces NASA’s Mars rover program and highlights the discoveries and engineering achievements that are shaping the future of space exploration.

Rovers

Curiosity

  • Curiosity is a car-sized rover designed to explore Gale Crater and study Mars’ climate and geology.
  • It has found evidence that ancient Mars may have had the right conditions to support microbial life.

Perseverance

  • Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater in 2021 to search for signs of ancient life.
  • It collects rock and soil samples that may be returned to Earth in future missions.
